# Trip Shapes — Brainstorming

No decisions here — just the option space laid out so we can mix and match.
The trip has four independent axes: **route in**, **Legoland time**, **add-ons**,
and **route out**. Pick one from each to form a complete itinerary.

---

## Axis 1 — Route in (Lansing → Goshen)

### A · Straight shot (~11–12 hrs, 1 driving day)
Leave early, arrive Goshen evening. Long day but gets you there fast.
Works best if the trip is short and every day counts.

### B · Split drive — Erie overnight (~2 days)
Day 1: Lansing → Erie, PA (~4.5 hrs). Stretch at Presque Isle State Park
(beach, free, easy). Overnight Erie.
Day 2: Erie → Goshen (~7 hrs). Arrive mid-afternoon.

### C · Split drive — Buffalo/Niagara overnight (~2 days)
Day 1: Lansing → Buffalo (~5 hrs). Overnight Buffalo.
Day 2: Morning at Niagara Falls (Maid of the Mist, Cave of the Winds) → drive
to Goshen (~4.5 hrs). Arrive early evening.
Adds the most "wow" factor on the drive in; adds ~1 hr vs. Erie routing.

### D · Split drive — Rochester overnight (~2 days)
Day 1: Lansing → Rochester (~7 hrs). Evening Strong Museum of Play (open late
some days) or just rest.
Day 2: Strong Museum of Play morning → Goshen (~2 hrs). Arrive by noon.
Gets the kids a big activity before Legoland; short second driving day.

### E · Southern route — Pittsburgh overnight (~2 days)
Day 1: Lansing → Pittsburgh (~5 hrs, via I-69/I-70). Mister Rogers Neighborhood
Museum + explore the city.
Day 2: Pittsburgh → Goshen (~6.5 hrs via PA Turnpike/I-78).
Completely different corridor from the northern I-90 options; good pairing with
a northern return route for maximum variety.

## Axis 2 — Time at Legoland

### 1 · One day
Doable if budget or schedule is tight. Rushed — Avery will want to repeat rides.
Best paired with on-site hotel for early entry.

### 2 · Two days *(likely sweet spot)*
Day 1 covers the park fully; Day 2 for favorites, waterpark, anything missed.
Avery's age is exactly what this park is built for — she'll max out 2 days.
Oscar and Nigel will feel the fatigue by day 2 — plan an add-on for them.

### 3 · Two days + waterpark focus
Same as above but Day 2 is primarily the on-site waterpark.
Good for July heat. Waterpark is included with some ticket combos.

---

## Axis 3 — Add-ons near Legoland (mix and match)

These are half-day or full-day options within 20–30 min of Goshen. Pick 0–2
depending on how many days you're in the area.

### 🗿 Storm King Art Center (Cornwall-on-Hudson — 15 min)
Outdoor sculpture park on 500 acres. Genuinely stunning; all ages but especially
Oscar and Nigel. Half-day or full day. Worth it.

### 🏕️ Harriman State Park (20 min)
Trails, lakes, swimming holes. Good decompression day after Legoland.
Pine Meadow Lake is an easy family hike with a payoff swim.

### ⚓ West Point (20 min)
Free tours, museum, overlook above the Hudson. Oscar-friendly.
Combine with a Hudson River walk in Cold Spring or Beacon.

### 🌆 NYC day trip (60 miles south — 90 min)
Full day commitment. Best if Oscar/Nigel specifically want it.
Central Park, a specific museum, pizza. Not essential given everything else nearby.

## Axis 4 — Route out (Goshen → Lansing)

### A · Straight shot
Mirror of route in. Long but gets everyone home.

### B · Strong Museum of Play stop (Rochester)
~2 hrs from Goshen; break up the drive with a 2–3 hr museum visit.
Works especially well if you didn't do Rochester on the way in.

### C · Niagara on the way out
If you didn't do Niagara on the way in, it works equally well returning.
Overnight Buffalo → Niagara morning → home.

### D · Different from route in
E.g., in via Buffalo/Niagara, out via Rochester Strong Museum.
Maximizes new experiences both directions.

### E · Southern return via Scranton (I-84 → I-81 → I-80)
Goshen → Scranton (~1.5 hrs) for Electric City Trolley Museum → home via I-80 (~9 hrs).
Slightly longer than I-90 but a completely different corridor — no repeated scenery,
and Scranton is a natural midpoint break.
