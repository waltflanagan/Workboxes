## 2026-02-11

**HCH** — (detailed notes — shame, integration, compartmentalization)
https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/helping-couples-heal-podcast/id1462962051?i=1000749158347

**Shame** — I am bad → I did harm

**Fragmented self** — Secret Basement. Integration work is necessary with a life of integrity.

**Fear of being fully known** — Private feeling management and validation seeking elsewhere. Feeling known once meant being hurt. Deep fear of being fully seen.

**Unmet Attachment needs** — Validation, reassurance, worth, belonging. Partner: "why wasn't I enough?" Name and express needs instead of acting out.

**Emotional Immaturity** — Haven't learned how to sit with discomfort.

**Compartmentalization as a survival strategy** — Automatic and unconscious ("I never wanted to hurt you"). Acting out piece separate from in-love piece. Partner feels gaslit from lack of apparent intent.

**Conflict Avoidance** — Power of positive confrontation. Very much tried to manage your emotions/reactions.

**Loss of integrity and self trust** — "I don't recognize myself." "I become someone I never wanted to be." Other trust can't happen if you can't self-trust. CONSISTENT VALUE-ALIGNED BEHAVIOR OVER TIME.

**Isolation after discovery** — Get into groups. Discovery creates isolation and that doesn't deepen accountability.

**Grief and remorse** — Grief cannot be accessed while shame and defense are there. Delay may be interpreted as lack of caring/remorse/empathy.

**Conclusion:** Betrayed partners heal through safety, truth, and consistency. Betraying partners need shame resilience, integration, and accountability. Unhealed wounds break relationships.
