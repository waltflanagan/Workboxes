# FIFA World Cup 2026 — Schedule for Trip Planning

Three of our destination cities are World Cup host cities: **New York/NJ**,
**Philadelphia**, and **Boston**. Match days = inflated hotel prices, traffic,
and crowds. The trip is July/August — below are the July dates that matter most.

---

## ⚠️ High-impact July dates

| Date | City | Match | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **July 4** | Philadelphia | Round of 16 @ Lincoln Financial Field, 5pm ET | 🔴 Extreme — also America's 250th anniversary; double crowd event |
| **July 5** | New York / NJ | Round of 16 @ MetLife, 4pm ET | 🔴 High — avoid NYC overnight this weekend |
| **July 9** | Boston | Quarterfinal @ Gillette Stadium (Foxborough), 4pm ET | 🟠 Significant — affects Boston-area lodging |
| **July 19** | New York / NJ | **FINAL @ MetLife Stadium, 3pm ET** | 🔴 Extreme — biggest single hotel price spike in the NYC metro; book nowhere near this date |

## Trip planning implications

- **Avoid July 4 weekend in Philadelphia** — Round of 16 + America's 250th = impossible crowds and hotel prices
- **Avoid July 19 weekend in NYC** — World Cup Final at MetLife; most expensive hotel weekend of the year in the metro area
- **Avoid July 9 in Boston area** — Quarterfinal; book lodging well clear of that date if visiting
- **June group stage dates** are less impactful for lodging but still mean crowds near stadiums on match days
- **Early August** is wide open in all three cities — if flexibility exists, push city visits to August
