# Boston, MA

## Overview
Rich history, walkable waterfront, excellent museums. Strong picks for all three kids.
~4 hrs from Goshen, NY — more of an overnight/2-night destination than a day trip.

---

## Activities

### 🔬 Museum of Science
- 1 Science Park, Boston, MA 02114
- 700+ interactive exhibits; Hayden Planetarium and Omni IMAX theater are standout add-ons
- Good for all three; especially Oscar (14) and Nigel (10)
- Est. ~$177 general admission (2 adults + 3 kids); theater shows extra ~$10–12/person
- CityPASS covers this — worthwhile if hitting 3–4 paid attractions
- [mos.org](https://www.mos.org)

### 🐟 New England Aquarium
- 1 Central Wharf, Boston, MA 02110
- Four-story 200,000-gallon Giant Ocean Tank; penguins, stingrays, touch tanks
- Avery (7) will be most delighted; works for all ages
- Est. ~$126–139; buy online in advance — summer lines are brutal
- [neaq.org](https://www.neaq.org)

### 🚶 Freedom Trail
- Starts at 139 Tremont St (Boston Common Visitor Center)
- 2.5-mile self-guided walk connecting 16 Revolutionary War sites; red line on pavement
- Ends at USS Constitution in Charlestown; NPS ranger tours are free
- Best for Oscar (14) and Nigel (10); Avery (7) may need motivation
- Est. free (individual paid sites $0–$10 each; budget $20–40 if going inside a few)
- [thefreedomtrail.org](https://www.thefreedomtrail.org)

### ⚓ USS Constitution + Museum (Charlestown Navy Yard)
- Charlestown Navy Yard, Building 22, Charlestown, MA 02129
- World's oldest commissioned warship still afloat; hands-on museum with mast-climbing sim
- Ship tour is free (run by US Navy); museum is suggested donation $10–20/adult
- All ages; Oscar (14) will appreciate the scale and naval history
- Est. free–$50 depending on donation; [ussconstitutionmuseum.org](https://ussconstitutionmuseum.org)
- *(Pairs naturally with Freedom Trail — both in Charlestown)*

### 🫖 Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum
- 306 Congress St, Boston, MA 02210
- Actor-led immersive reenactment; visitors "throw tea" overboard from replica ships
- Best for Oscar (14) and Nigel (10); intensity may be much for Avery (7)
- Est. ~$157–174; strollers not allowed
- [bostonteapartyship.com](https://www.bostonteapartyship.com)

### 🏝️ Boston Harbor Islands (Spectacle or Georges Island)
- Ferry from Long Wharf North, 200 Atlantic Ave, Boston, MA 02110
- Spectacle Island: lifeguarded beach, hiking, sea glass, city views
- Georges Island: Civil War-era Fort Warren to explore
- Great for all three; 10- and 7-year-old love the beach; Oscar will enjoy the fort and hike
- Est. ~$122 round-trip ferry (2 adults + 3 kids); islands are free
- [bostonharborislands.org](https://www.bostonharborislands.org)

### ⚾ Fenway Park Tour
- 4 Jersey St (Gate E), Boston, MA 02215
- Guided tour of America's oldest ballpark — Green Monster seats, press box, 170,000+ artifacts
- Best for Oscar (14); Nigel (10) will like it; more interesting if any sports interest
- Est. ~$91–99 for family of 5
- [mlb.com/redsox/ballpark/tours](https://www.mlb.com/redsox/ballpark/tours)

### 🦆 Boston Duck Tours
- Departs from Museum of Science and Prudential Center
- 80-min amphibious WWII vehicle tour of landmarks, ends with a Charles River splash
- Best for Avery (7) and Nigel (10); novelty factor for Oscar too
- Est. ~$190–195 for family of 5; books up fast in summer — reserve well in advance
- [bostonducktours.com](https://bostonducktours.com)

### 🧒 Boston Children's Museum
- 308 Congress St, Boston, MA 02210 (waterfront)
- Three floors: construction zone, Japanese house, climbing structure, science experiments
- Best for Avery (7); Nigel (10) will still enjoy it; Oscar (14) may find it young
- Est. ~$110–120 regular; **$5 total on Sunday afternoons 1–4pm** — plan around this
- [bostonchildrensmuseum.org](https://bostonchildrensmuseum.org)

### 🌸 Boston Public Garden + Swan Boats
- 4 Charles St, Boston, MA 02116
- America's first public botanical garden; pedal-powered Swan Boats since 1877
- Relaxed half-hour stop; pairs with Boston Common and Frog Pond spray pool
- Best for Avery (7); pleasant for all as a breather between bigger stops
- Est. ~$20 for Swan Boats; garden is free
- [swanboats.com](https://swanboats.com)

### 🎓 Harvard Square + Harvard Natural History Museum (Cambridge)
- Museum: 26 Oxford St, Cambridge, MA 02138 (short T ride across the river)
- Harvard Square is a lively, independent neighborhood — bookstores, food, street performers
- Museum has the famous Glass Flowers collection, dinosaurs, minerals
- Oscar (14) will appreciate the Square vibe; museum works for all three
- Est. ~$90 for museum (family of 5); Square is free
- [hmnh.harvard.edu](https://hmnh.harvard.edu)

### 🌿 Rose Kennedy Greenway
- Atlantic Ave & Surface Rd, Boston, MA 02110 (runs ~1.5 miles through downtown)
- Linear park with rotating public art, free outdoor concerts, carousel, food trucks
- Easy connector between waterfront stops; North End (Italian neighborhood) is right here
- Free; all ages
- [rosekennedygreenway.org](https://www.rosekennedygreenway.org)

### 🦁 Franklin Park Zoo
- 1 Franklin Park Road, Boston, MA 02121
- 72-acre zoo in Olmsted's Franklin Park; gorillas, lions, giraffes, African Tropical Forest
- Half-day manageable; less crowded than waterfront attractions
- Best for Avery (7) and Nigel (10)
- Est. ~$100 (dynamic pricing; buy in advance); [zoonewengland.org](https://www.zoonewengland.org/franklin-park-zoo)

---

## Value notes
- **CityPASS** (~$84/adult, ~$72/child): covers Museum of Science, Aquarium, Franklin Park Zoo + 2 more — saves ~45% if hitting 4+
- **Freedom Trail + USS Constitution + Charlestown Navy Yard** is one full walkable day, mostly free
- **Harbor Islands + Boston Common/Public Garden/Greenway** is a great all-outdoor day
- **Sunday afternoon at the Children's Museum** ($5 total for family) is worth planning around if Avery is the priority
