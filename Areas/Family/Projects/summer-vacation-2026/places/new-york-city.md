# New York City, NY

## Overview
~60 miles / 90 min south of Goshen — easy day trip or overnight from Legoland base.
Free or low-cost options are plentiful. ⚠️ See `world-cup-schedule.md` — World Cup Final
is at MetLife Stadium (NJ) on July 26; hotel prices around that date will be extreme.

---

## Activities

### 🗽 Staten Island Ferry
- Departs Whitehall Terminal, 4 Whitehall St, Lower Manhattan, NY 10004
- 25-min free ferry through New York Harbor — unobstructed Statue of Liberty views, Manhattan skyline, Verrazano Bridge. Departs every 15–30 min; ride both ways and you're back in under an hour.
- All ages; even skeptical teens enjoy the harbor crossing
- **Free** — [siferry.com](https://siferry.com)

### 🏝️ Governors Island
- Ferry from Pier 6, Battery Maritime Building, 10 South St, Manhattan
- Car-free island open Memorial Day–Labor Day. 70-ft Outlook Hill, 57-ft slide, hammocks, water jets, food, public art. One of the best low-key summer days in the city.
- All ages
- Est. ~$10 (2 adult ferry tickets; kids 12 and under free; free before 11am weekends)
- [govisland.com](https://www.govisland.com)

### 🎡 Coney Island / Luna Park
- 1000 Surf Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11224
- Classic seaside boardwalk — the Cyclone wooden roller coaster, Nathan's Famous hot dogs, arcades, real beach alongside. No general admission; pay-per-ride or all-day wristband.
- All ages; Oscar (14) will appreciate the Cyclone and boardwalk freedom; Avery (7) has kiddie rides
- Est. ~$150–200 for family wristbands + food; beach is always free
- Best on a weekday — [lunaparknyc.com](https://lunaparknyc.com)

### 🚇 New York Transit Museum
- Corner of Boerum Pl & Schermerhorn St, Brooklyn Heights, Brooklyn, NY 11201
- Built inside a decommissioned 1936 subway station. Vintage subway cars from the 1900s–70s you can walk and sit in; signal tower, history of subway construction. One of NYC's underrated gems.
- Avery (7) and Nigel (10) especially; transportation-curious teens will enjoy it too
- Est. ~$35 (adults $10, kids 2–17 $5) — [nytransitmuseum.org](https://www.nytransitmuseum.org)

### ✈️ Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum
- Pier 86, W 46th St & 12th Ave, New York, NY 10036
- Decommissioned WWII aircraft carrier with dozens of military aircraft on deck, Space Shuttle Enterprise pavilion, and submarine USS Growler. Hands-on and visually dramatic — hard to find boring.
- All ages; Oscar (14) and Nigel (10) will get the most from Space Shuttle and aircraft exhibits
- Est. ~$170 for family of 5 — [intrepidmuseum.org](https://intrepidmuseum.org)

### 🎨 The High Line + Chelsea Market
- High Line: Gansevoort St to 34th St, West Side Manhattan (multiple access points)
- 1.45-mile elevated linear park on a former freight rail line. Public art, native plants, food vendors, free live music all summer. One block from Chelsea Market (75 9th Ave) — 50+ food vendors in the old Nabisco factory building.
- All ages; natural connector between neighborhoods
- High Line: **free** / Chelsea Market: free entry, ~$60–100 for family lunch
- [thehighline.org](https://www.thehighline.org) / [chelseamarket.com](https://www.chelseamarket.com)

### 🦕 American Museum of Natural History
- Central Park West & 79th St, New York, NY 10024
- One of the world's great natural history museums — dinosaurs, ocean life, cosmos, human origins. Rose Center for Earth and Space and Hayden Planetarium are standouts. Plan 3–4 hours.
- All ages; especially Nigel (10) and Oscar (14) for the science depth
- Est. ~$140 for family of 5 — [amnh.org](https://www.amnh.org)

### 🏛️ The Metropolitan Museum of Art
- 1000 Fifth Ave, New York, NY 10028
- Largest art museum in the Western Hemisphere. Egyptian temple, arms and armor hall, and rooftop garden are family favorites. Kids 12 and under free; under-18 free for NY residents.
- Best for Oscar (14); Nigel (10) also engaged; Egyptian wing works for Avery (7)
- Est. ~$60–90 depending on how the 14-year-old's age is counted — [metmuseum.org](https://www.metmuseum.org)

### 🌆 Top of the Rock (Rockefeller Center)
- 30 Rockefeller Plaza, New York, NY 10112
- 70th-floor open-air observation deck — arguably better views than Empire State (you can see ESB from here). Three levels including glass-enclosed option. A genuine "wow" moment.
- All ages; the city-at-scale view impresses everyone including a skeptical 14-year-old
- Est. ~$203 for family of 5; book ahead — time-slot based, cheaper on weekday mornings
- [rockefellercenter.com](https://www.rockefellercenter.com/tickets/top-of-the-rock-observation-deck)

### 🎪 Brooklyn Bridge Park + DUMBO
- Main entrance: Main St & Plymouth St, Brooklyn, NY 11201
- 1.3-mile waterfront park under the bridges; playgrounds, sports courts, lawns, stunning skyline views. Jane's Carousel ($2/ride) in a glass pavilion at water's edge. DUMBO's brick streets have the famous Manhattan Bridge arch photo. Brooklyn Flea runs weekends.
- All ages; Avery (7) loves playground + carousel; Oscar (14) enjoys the independent neighborhood feel
- Park and DUMBO: **free** (carousel $2/ride) — [brooklynbridgepark.org](https://brooklynbridgepark.org)

### 🐘 Bronx Zoo
- 2300 Southern Blvd, Bronx, NY 10460
- One of the world's largest metropolitan zoos — 6,000+ animals, 265 acres. Tiger Mountain, Congo Gorilla Forest, Children's Zoo. **Wednesdays: free limited admission** (reserve ahead — fills up in summer).
- All ages; Avery (7) and Nigel (10) especially
- Est. ~$121+ at peak summer flex pricing; buy ahead for best rates; Wednesdays free
- [bronxzoo.com](https://bronxzoo.com)

### 🌐 ARTECHOUSE NYC
- 439 W 15th St, New York, NY 10011 (Chelsea Market building)
- Large-scale immersive digital art environments — walked through and interacted with. Installations change seasonally. Genuinely cool and a strong pick for a 14-year-old.
- Oscar (14) especially; works for all ages
- Est. ~$150–175 for family of 5 — [artechouse.com](https://www.artechouse.com/location/nyc/)

## Free or near-free highlights
- Staten Island Ferry — free
- Governors Island — free island; ferry ~$10 adults (free before 11am weekends)
- The High Line — free
- Brooklyn Bridge Park + DUMBO — free
- Bronx Zoo Wednesdays — free limited admission (reserve in advance)
- The Met — free under 12; NY residents pay what they wish

## Logistics
- Drive and park (~$40–60/day) or take Metro-North from Orange County area stations (~1 hr)
- Train is lower stress with kids; highly recommended over driving into Manhattan
