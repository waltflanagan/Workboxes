# Philadelphia, PA

## Overview
~2 hrs from Goshen, NY. America's birthplace — free history, great food, surprising depth.
Strong value: many of the best stops are free or very cheap.
⚠️ See `world-cup-schedule.md` — Philadelphia is a World Cup host city; match days mean
inflated hotel prices and crowds.

---

## Activities

### 🔔 Liberty Bell Center
- 526 Market St, Philadelphia, PA 19106
- Glass pavilion on Independence Mall with exhibits on the bell's history and its connection to the abolitionist movement. Summer hours 9am–7pm; arrive early or mid-afternoon to beat lines. Security screening required.
- All ages; the 10- and 14-year-old will get more from the exhibit context
- **Free** — [nps.gov/inde](https://www.nps.gov/inde/planyourvisit/libertybellcenter.htm)

### 🏛️ Independence Hall
- 520 Chestnut St, Philadelphia, PA 19106
- Where the Declaration of Independence and Constitution were signed. Guided tours through the Assembly Room where the Founders worked. Timed entry; **must reserve in advance at Recreation.gov** — sells out weeks ahead in summer.
- Best for Oscar (14) and Nigel (10); the 7-year-old may find the hour-long guided format slow
- Est. $5 total (just the $1/ticket reservation handling fee — tour is free)
- [nps.gov/inde](https://www.nps.gov/inde/planyourvisit/independencehall.htm)

### 📜 National Constitution Center
- 525 Arch St, Philadelphia, PA 19106 (Independence Mall)
- Interactive history museum with "Freedom Rising" multimedia show and Signers' Hall — life-size bronze statues of all the Founders you can walk among. New galleries opening for America's 250th anniversary in 2026. One of the strongest picks for Oscar (14).
- Best for Oscar (14) and Nigel (10); Avery (7) will enjoy the bronze statues and theatrical show
- Est. ~$41 (2 adults x $15 + 3 kids x $11) — [constitutioncenter.org](https://constitutioncenter.org)

### 🔬 The Franklin Institute
- 271 N. 21st St, Philadelphia, PA 19103
- Philadelphia's flagship science museum — space, electricity, engineering, the human body, Giant Heart walk-through. Planetarium and IMAX on-site as add-ons. Tickets valid all day with re-entry.
- All ages; Oscar (14) especially for engineering and space exhibits
- Est. ~$125 general admission (planetarium/IMAX extra ~$8–12/person)
- [fi.edu](https://www.fi.edu)

### 🏚️ Eastern State Penitentiary
- 2027 Fairmount Ave, Philadelphia, PA 19130
- Crumbling former prison (1829–1971) that once held Al Capone. Self-guided audio tour through cellblocks, guard towers, and art installations. Gothic architecture; atmospheric and genuinely memorable. One of the most distinctive attractions in the city.
- Oscar (14) will love it; Nigel (10) should be fine; use judgment for Avery (7) — tone is dark/eerie
- Est. ~$94 for family of 5; buy online to avoid $2/ticket door surcharge
- [easternstate.org](https://www.easternstate.org)

### 🎨 Philadelphia Museum of Art + Rocky Steps
- 2600 Benjamin Franklin Pkwy, Philadelphia, PA 19130
- The "Rocky Steps" — running up and striking the pose at the top is a Philadelphia rite of passage. Rocky bronze statue at the base. World-class art collection inside. Kids and teens under 19 are **free**.
- Rocky Steps: all ages; museum best for Oscar (14) and adults
- Est. ~$50 (2 adults x $25; under-19 free); Rocky Steps always free even without entering
- **Friday evenings (summer 2026) are pay-what-you-wish** — [philamuseum.org](https://www.philamuseum.org)

### ⛵ Independence Seaport Museum
- 211 S. Christopher Columbus Blvd, Philadelphia, PA 19106 (Penn's Landing)
- Maritime museum on the Delaware River. General admission includes boarding USS Olympia, a Spanish-American War-era warship. Submarine USS Becuna available as guided add-on tour (11am, 1pm, 3pm).
- Best for Nigel (10) and Oscar (14); Avery (7) will enjoy climbing the warship
- Est. ~$55 (2 adults x $15 + 3 kids x $10; sub tour extra) — [phillyseaport.org](https://www.phillyseaport.org)

### 🦒 Philadelphia Zoo
- 3400 W. Girard Ave, Philadelphia, PA 19104 (Fairmount Park)
- America's first zoo (1874), 42 acres, 1,300+ animals. Standout feature: **Zoo360** — elevated mesh trails letting big cats and apes travel over visitors' heads. Genuinely impressive at any age.
- All ages; Zoo360 will impress even Oscar (14)
- Est. ~$100–120 (dynamic pricing; buy early online for best rate); credit card only, no cash
- [philadelphiazoo.org](https://www.philadelphiazoo.org)

### 🥩 Reading Terminal Market
- 51 N. 12th St, Philadelphia, PA 19107
- One of the oldest and largest public markets in the US (1893). 80+ vendors: PA Dutch/Amish farmers, Philly cheesesteak, Bassetts Ice Cream (open since 1892), Beiler's Doughnuts made by hand. Voted Best Public Market in America 2026.
- All ages; easy and informal with kids
- **Free entry**; budget ~$10–20/person for food — [readingterminalmarket.org](https://readingterminalmarket.org)
- *(Note: many Amish vendors are cash-only)*

### 🥖 Pat's vs. Geno's Cheesesteak Crawl
- Pat's: 1237 E. Passyunk Ave / Geno's: 1219 S. 9th St (directly across the street)
- The two most legendary rival cheesesteak institutions in Philadelphia, on opposite corners of the same intersection in South Philly. Order from both, compare, pick a side. Counter-service, outdoor seating, no-frills.
- All ages; Oscar (14) especially will appreciate the rivalry history
- Free entry; budget ~$12–15/person — [patskingofsteaks.com](https://www.patskingofsteaks.com) / [genossteaks.com](https://www.genossteaks.com)

### 🏛️ Benjamin Franklin Museum
- 317 Chestnut St, Philadelphia, PA 19106 (Franklin Court, near Independence Mall)
- Underground museum beneath the original site of Franklin's home. Covers his life as inventor, printer, diplomat, scientist. Ghost structure of his home preserved above ground. Compact and well-done; pairs naturally with Liberty Bell/Independence Hall (short walk).
- Best for Nigel (10) and Oscar (14); Avery (7) will enjoy the hands-on invention exhibits
- Est. ~$16 (adults $5, kids $2) — [nps.gov/inde](https://www.nps.gov/inde/planyourvisit/benjaminfranklinmuseum.htm)

### 🛝 Smith Memorial Playground
- 3500 Reservoir Drive, East Fairmount Park, Philadelphia, PA 19121
- Historic 6-acre outdoor playground (operating since 1899) with a **44-foot wooden slide**. The Playhouse is 16,000 sq ft of indoor play for kids 10 and under — tumbling room, indoor tricycle track, climb-on train.
- Best for Avery (7) and Nigel (10); the 44-ft slide is fun at any age
- **Free** — [smithplayground.org](https://www.smithplayground.org)
- *(Closed Mondays; bring snacks — no food on site)*

### 🎠 Please Touch Museum
- 4231 Avenue of the Republic, Philadelphia, PA 19131 (West Fairmount Park)
- Children's museum in a stunning 1876 Centennial Exhibition building. Hands-on exhibits, restored antique carousel. Skews younger but the 10-year-old will still find plenty.
- Best for Avery (7); Nigel (10) will enjoy it; Oscar (14) will likely be bored
- Est. ~$95 (all ages $19); **last Sunday of June/July/August is free** (reserve ahead)
- [pleasetouchmuseum.org](https://www.pleasetouchmuseum.org)

## Free or near-free highlights
- Liberty Bell Center — free
- Independence Hall — essentially free ($1/ticket reservation fee)
- Rocky Steps (PMA exterior) — always free
- Reading Terminal Market — free entry
- Pat's + Geno's — free entry
- Smith Memorial Playground — free
- Benjamin Franklin Museum — ~$16 for family of 5

## Best single-day combo (history focus)
Liberty Bell + Independence Hall (book ahead) → Benjamin Franklin Museum → National Constitution Center → Reading Terminal Market for lunch. All within walking distance on Independence Mall.
